Teeth-Whitening

Tooth whitening, also known as bleaching, is a straightforward and painless dental procedure used to improve the color of your natural tooth enamel. It's a great way to enhance the beauty of your smile. Because tooth whitening specifically targets the natural tooth surface, it's important to consider replacing any old dental work like fillings and crowns, if needed. We typically replace these restorations after the whitening process to ensure they match your newly brightened teeth.

Dental Implant

A dental implant is a permanent solution for replacing a missing tooth. It feels and looks just like a real tooth because it becomes a part of your jawbone. Unlike bridges or dentures, dental implants also help prevent bone loss and gum problems. They're a great choice for people with good overall and oral health who have lost a tooth due to gum disease or injury. Dental implants are one of the most advanced dental options available today and are known for their high success rates.

Scaling & Polishing

Scaling & Polishing is a painless process that removes plaque and tartar, which can cause gum issues. It's used to treat gingivitis and periodontal disease. Using a special tool, we clean below the gum line and may apply an antimicrobial agent. Then, we polish your teeth and floss to ensure they're thoroughly clean.

Braces & Aligners

Braces have been a trusted method for fixing crooked teeth and misaligned bites for years. They involve attaching metal brackets to your teeth and connecting them with wires. While effective, traditional braces can be quite noticeable and deter some people from seeking treatment. We're excited to offer an alternative – clear braces for both adults and teens. These braces work just as well as metal ones but are nearly invisible. They're perfect for individuals, including professionals and teens, who want to improve their smile without the obvious appearance of traditional braces. Clear braces function similarly to regular braces, with brackets on each tooth and a wire connecting them. The key difference is that these brackets and wires are made from tooth-colored material, making them blend in with your teeth. However, it's important to keep them clean, as they can still stain.

Root Canal Treatment

Root canals are a relatively simple procedure, typically taking one to three office visits. The best part is that when needed, a root canal can save your tooth and preserve your smile! When a tooth has a deep cavity or is cracked, harmful bacteria can enter the pulp inside the tooth, leading to an infection. If not treated, this can result in pain, swelling, and potentially damage to your jawbone and overall health. Without proper care, the tooth may need to be removed. Signs that you might need a root canal include severe toothache, pain when chewing or applying pressure, heightened sensitivity to hot and cold, a darkened tooth, and gum swelling or tenderness. If you notice any of these symptoms, it's essential to contact your dentist promptly.

A dental filling is used to fix a tooth that's damaged by cracks, decay, or other issues that weaken it. In this procedure, the damaged part of the tooth is removed and replaced with a composite filling. These fillings are custom-made to match the color of your natural teeth, unlike older metal fillings.

Advanced Gum Treatment

Gum disease, or periodontitis, affects nearly half of Americans over 30, according to the CDC. Left untreated, it can lead to tooth loss. Fortunately, there are effective treatments available today that can help you keep your teeth. This article covers the signs, risk factors, and treatment options for gum disease, giving you the confidence to make informed decisions about your oral health. The disease begins with gingivitis, caused by plaque buildup on your teeth. If not treated, it can progress to periodontitis, damaging your gums, bone, and tooth-supporting ligaments. In severe cases, teeth may become loose and require removal. However, even in advanced cases, tooth loss can be prevented. Seeking treatment early greatly improves your chances of saving your teeth and maintaining good overall health.
